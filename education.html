Moving to the Marriott School of business

I'm super excited to be studying here at BYU for my undergraduate degree! Originally, when I applied to BYU in 2023, I was enrolled in the College of Fine Arts to pursue a degree in Theater Education. However, after re-evaluating what I wanted my lifestyle and career to look like, I decided to pivot to pursue Human Resource Management. I adored theatre in high school, and I still do! I was a stage manager for four years, where I developed organization and interpersonal skills that helped me fall in love with helping and supporting others to shine. I decided that although I do love theatre, I want to enter a career field that has the potential for remote work so I have the opportunity to stay home and raise my children. I was also finding myself without the intellecutal challenge I thrive on when using analytical perspectives. I reflected on the digital and communication skills I've developed, the empathy I have for others and the passion I have for digital systems. What I realized is that many of the skills I used in my theatrical work as a stage manager would transfer wonderfully to human resource management! I love teaching others, training them, defining and refining positions within a workplace, and creating systems that allow others to fulfill their passion more efficiently.

Learning so Far

I declared myself a pre-business major in August of 2024, with the intent of applying to BYU's HRM program in June of 2025. So far, I have had the opportunity to take a series of business pre-requisites that have taught me many invaluable skills and attitudes! I have resparked my inclination for analytical thinking in Statistics, accounting, marketing, and information systems classes. I am eagerly absorbing the knowledge from those around me and finding connections across subjects/fields, and building entirely new skillsets (see skills for more).

Going Forward

I strive to listen and understand other peoples' motives and goals, and to help them find the right environment to thrive. With this drive, I know I will be a successful HR manager! However, I have a lot to learn before I get there. I actively seek opportunities to learn of things that inherently pertain to HRM and things that don't. I believe being well-rounded is an invaluable characteristic that allows people to thrive in various aspects of our lives. Above all else, I seek to love all people the way Jesus Christ does. I have a fundemental belief in His love for us, and His desire for us to be successful. This is ultimately what drives me to seek understanding and growth in myself and others.
